# Authorization, Intended Use & Legal Notice

This software is built for **defensive security research, monitoring, and education** on systems and networks that you own or are explicitly authorized to operate.

## Intended Use

Meli is a personal defensive-security and home-lab tool. It is designed to be run by its operator against their own infrastructure — their own honeypots, their own network, their own devices, their own lab environment. That is the only use it is designed, intended, or supported for.

Specifically, the components of this project are intended to:

- Monitor honeypots and decoy services that you have deployed on infrastructure you control, and analyze the activity of attackers who choose to interact with those decoys.
- Capture, analyze, and visualize traffic, devices, and activity on networks you own or administer.
- Provide threat-intelligence enrichment, alerting, reporting, and visualization for your own security monitoring.

## What This Software Is NOT For

This software is **not** intended for, and must not be used for:

- Monitoring, capturing, intercepting, or analyzing traffic on networks you do not own or are not explicitly authorized to monitor.
- Surveilling, tracking, profiling, or collecting data about other people, their devices, or their communications without their knowledge and consent.
- Scanning, probing, exploiting, attacking, or accessing any system, network, device, or account that you do not own or have explicit written authorization to test.
- Any activity that violates applicable local, state, federal, or international law.

## Your Responsibility

You — the operator — are solely responsible for ensuring your use of this software is lawful in your jurisdiction and authorized for the systems and networks you point it at.

Laws governing network monitoring, packet capture, wireless interception, and security testing vary significantly by location and can include (among others) computer-misuse statutes, wiretapping and electronic-communications-privacy laws, and unauthorized-access laws. Passive packet capture or wireless/RF scanning that is perfectly legal on your own network can be a serious offense on a network you do not control.

**When in doubt, don't.** Get written authorization first, or limit the tool to infrastructure you unambiguously own.
